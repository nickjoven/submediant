# Glossary

Terms are defined in the context of the synchronization framework. Standard physics definitions may be narrower.

---

a₀ (MOND acceleration scale)
: The acceleration below which Newtonian gravity fails and the MOND regime begins. In this framework, $a_0 = cH_0/(2\pi)$: the threshold where gravitational coupling drops below the synchronization critical point.

ADM formalism
: The Arnowitt-Deser-Misner decomposition of spacetime into spatial slices evolving in time. Provides the lapse function, shift vector, and extrinsic curvature that the framework reinterprets as synchronization variables.

Arnold tongues
: Wedge-shaped regions in parameter space where a driven oscillator locks to a rational frequency ratio $p/q$. In the framework, these encode the mode-locking structure of the Stern-Brocot tree on the unit circle.

Bifurcation
: A qualitative change in system behavior as a parameter crosses a threshold. The MOND boundary ($x = K/K_c = 1$) is a transcritical bifurcation where the synchronization fixed point vanishes.

Born rule
: The quantum mechanical rule that probability equals the squared amplitude of the wave function. Derived here as the basin measure of the Ott-Antonsen fixed point: the fraction of initial conditions that flow to a given attractor scales as $|z|^2$.

Coherence $r(x,t)$
: The Kuramoto order parameter. Measures the degree of phase agreement among coupled oscillators: $r = 1$ is perfect synchronization, $r = 0$ is incoherence. In the gravitational reading, $r$ maps to the metric determinant.

Continuum limit
: The passage from discrete rational structure to continuous fields. The framework identifies two: $K > K_c$ yields Einstein's field equations (the gravitational/classical limit); $K < K_c$ yields Schrödinger's equation (the quantum limit).

Coupling strength $K$
: The parameter in the Kuramoto model controlling how strongly oscillators pull each other toward phase alignment. In the gravitational reading, $K$ maps to the medium's impedance — the capacity of spacetime to transmit synchronization.

Dark matter dual
: The subharmonic displacement variable in the stick-slip reading. Not a particle, but a bookkeeping variable: the difference between the baryonic prediction and the observed dynamics, which the framework derives as a necessary consequence of partial synchronization.

Devil's staircase
: A fractal step function that arises from mode-locking: as a driving parameter sweeps continuously, the system's frequency ratio jumps between rational plateaus. The Stern-Brocot tree indexes these plateaus.

Extrinsic curvature $K_{ij}$
: The rate of change of the spatial metric as it evolves between time slices in the ADM formalism. Reinterpreted as phase-weighted desynchronization: how fast different regions of space fall out of sync.

Farey sequence
: The ordered set of all reduced fractions with denominator at most $N$. Adjacent Farey fractions are Stern-Brocot neighbors and their mediants fill the next level of the tree.

Fidelity bound
: A self-referential constraint: the framework's own internal consistency requires that $a_0 = cH/(2\pi)$ at every epoch. This single condition unifies the MOND transition function, the Born rule normalization, and the collapse threshold.

Klein bottle
: A non-orientable surface that arises as the topology of the phase space when the circle map's orientation-reversing fixed point is included. Appears in the framework's treatment of spin-statistics and CPT symmetry.

Kuramoto model
: A mean-field model of $N$ coupled phase oscillators: $\dot{\theta}_i = \omega_i + (K/N)\sum_j \sin(\theta_j - \theta_i)$. The framework's starting point. All gravitational and quantum structure is derived from this equation and its reductions.

Lapse function $N$
: In ADM gravity, the rate at which proper time advances relative to coordinate time. Reinterpreted as the local coupling propagation speed — how fast synchronization signals travel through the medium.

Lyapunov functional
: An energy-like quantity that decreases monotonically along trajectories, proving convergence to equilibrium. The framework constructs one for the Kuramoto-Einstein system, guaranteeing that the synchronization reading of gravity has a unique, stable ground state.

MDAR (Mass Discrepancy-Acceleration Relation)
: The observed tight correlation between baryonic acceleration $g_{\text{bar}}$ and total observed acceleration $g_{\text{obs}}$ in galaxies. The framework derives this as a necessary consequence of the Ott-Antonsen reduction applied to the Kuramoto model on a disk.

Mediant
: Given fractions $a/b$ and $c/d$, their mediant is $(a+c)/(b+d)$. The fundamental operation of the Stern-Brocot tree. In the framework, one of four irreducible primitives from which all structure is built.

MOND (Modified Newtonian Dynamics)
: Milgrom's empirical observation (1983) that galaxy dynamics below acceleration $a_0$ follow $g_{\text{obs}} = \sqrt{g_{\text{bar}} \cdot a_0}$ rather than Newton's law. The framework derives MOND as the desynchronization regime of the Kuramoto model.

Ott-Antonsen reduction
: An exact dimensional reduction of the infinite-dimensional Kuramoto system to a single complex variable $z(t)$ on the unit disk. The framework's central technical tool: it converts the $N$-body synchronization problem into a tractable ODE.

Planck scale
: The energy scale ($\sim 10^{19}$ GeV) where quantum gravity effects become important. Derived here as the self-sustaining threshold: the coupling strength at which a single oscillator can maintain coherence against its own decoherence.

Proslambenomenos
: Greek: "the added tone." The lowest note of the ancient Greek musical system, added to complete the double octave. In this framework, the cosmological constant $\Lambda$ — the reference frequency from which all other scales (Hubble rate, MOND threshold, Planck scale) descend as overtones.

Rational field equation
: The self-consistency condition on the Stern-Brocot tree: a field on rationals satisfying $f(p/q) = F(f(a/b), f(c/d))$ where $a/b, c/d$ are the Stern-Brocot parents of $p/q$. The discrete precursor of Einstein's field equations.

Renzo's Rule
: The empirical observation (Sancisi 2004) that every feature in a galaxy's baryonic mass profile has a corresponding feature in its rotation curve. The framework derives this as a consequence of the Kuramoto self-consistency equation: the coupling (baryons) uniquely determines the coherence profile (rotation curve).

Shift vector $N^i$
: In ADM gravity, the coordinate drift between successive time slices. Reinterpreted as the rate at which synchronization phase fronts drift spatially — a "current" of desynchronization.

SL(2,$\mathbb{R}$)
: The special linear group of 2×2 real matrices with determinant 1. The symmetry group of the Stern-Brocot tree and of Möbius transformations on the upper half-plane. The framework's substrate Lie group, from which the mediant operation inherits its structure.

Spectral tilt $n_s$
: The deviation from exact scale-invariance in the CMB power spectrum ($n_s \approx 0.965$). Derived here from the mode-locking structure: the synchronization cost gradient across the devil's staircase produces a tilt of $1 - 1/N_{\text{efolds}}$.

Stern-Brocot tree
: The binary tree of all positive rationals, constructed by iterated mediants starting from $0/1$ and $1/0$. The framework's fundamental discrete structure: it organizes the rational frequency ratios that index Arnold tongues and mode-locked states.

Stribeck curve
: The empirical relationship between friction and sliding velocity: friction drops from a static peak, reaches a minimum, then rises again with velocity. The framework identifies this shape with the MOND interpolating function $\nu(x)$, unifying tribology and galactic dynamics.

Synchronization cost
: The "price" of maintaining coherence at a given mode-locking ratio $p/q$. Scales with the denominator $q$: simple ratios (1/1, 1/2) are cheap; complex ratios (13/21) are expensive. The cost landscape on the Stern-Brocot tree generates the framework's predictions.

Transcritical bifurcation
: The specific bifurcation type at $x = 1$ (the MOND boundary): the stable fixed point $r^* = \sqrt{1-1/x}$ collides with the unstable fixed point at $r = 0$ and they exchange stability. Marks the transition from Newtonian (synchronized) to MOND (desynchronized) gravity.
