Our Address on the Tree

Rational · $\theta = 0$
A tree that contains a universe

The Stern-Brocot tree is a binary tree containing every positive rational number exactly once. It is constructed by one operation: the mediant. Given fractions $a/b$ and $c/d$, their mediant is $(a+c)/(b+d)$. This is a theorem, not a hypothesis. The tree exists because the integers exist and the mediant is well-defined.

Whether the predictions match observation is an empirical question.

Time as an imaginary thread

The polynomial $x^2 - x - 1 = 0$ has two roots: $\varphi$ and $\psi = -1/\varphi$. Their product is $-1$. This algebraic fact — that the product of the two convergence directions equals the square of the imaginary unit — is what gives spacetime its $(3,1)$ signature. The recurrence approaches $1/\varphi$ from above (our root) and from below (the conjugate). Both roots share every gate crossing. The difference between them is the phase: the part of history that survives only in relationships, not in records.
