Three Bodies

The minimum system that produces all the framework's structure.

I

Three Bodies

$$\dot{\theta}_i = \omega_i + \frac{K}{3}\sum_{j=1}^{3} \sin(\theta_j - \theta_i), \qquad i = 1,2,3$$

Three oscillators. Three natural frequencies. One coupling strength. Two coupled oscillators always reduce to a single relative-phase equation, which is exactly solvable. Three is the minimum where the coupling topology forms a loop and the dynamics become irreducible. This is therefore the minimum system that exhibits synchronization, mode-locking, and chaos.

II

Why Three

  • Two bodies always reduce to a single relative phase. Trivially solvable.
  • Three bodies are the first system where the dynamics can't be decomposed. The Poincaré result (1890): no general closed-form solution exists.
  • But specific solutions do exist. The figure-8 (Chenciner–Montgomery, 2000): all three bodies chase each other around a single curve. Equal masses. Equal time intervals. The curve is a lemniscate variant.
  • In the framework: $d = 3$ because $\dim\,\mathrm{SL}(2) = 2^2 - 1 = 3$. Three spatial dimensions come from three being the minimum for non-degenerate mediant geometry.
III

What Emerges

From three coupled oscillators you get:

  • The Stern-Brocot tree (rational frequency ratios between the three)
  • Arnold tongues (mode-locking regions)
  • The devil's staircase (the winding number as a function of frequency ratio)
  • Chaos (between the tongues)
  • The figure-8 orbit (the stable solution at equal coupling)

This is the four primitives: integers (three of them), mediant (their frequency ratios), fixed point (the stable orbit), parabola (the bifurcation at the tongue boundaries).

IV

The Minimum

$$N = 3: \text{ the smallest system with non-trivial synchronization topology}$$

Two oscillators have one coupling. Three have three. The coupling graph is a triangle — the simplest closed loop. Self-consistency requires the loop: the state of oscillator 1 depends on 2, which depends on 3, which depends on 1. This circular dependency is the fixed-point condition $x = f(x)$.

V

Newtonian Limits

$$\ddot{\mathbf{r}}_i = -G\sum_{j \neq i} m_j \frac{\mathbf{r}_i - \mathbf{r}_j}{|\mathbf{r}_i - \mathbf{r}_j|^3}$$

The simulation above solves this equation. It works — the figure-8 orbit closes. But the equation encodes five assumptions that nature does not share:

  • Instantaneous action. The force between bodies propagates at infinite speed. Change a mass and every body in the universe knows immediately.
  • No back-reaction. Bodies curve nothing. Space is a fixed stage, indifferent to the masses moving through it.
  • Singular at contact. As $r \to 0$, the force diverges: $F \to \infty$. Newton gives no mechanism to regulate the collision.
  • No radiation. Accelerating masses emit no gravitational waves. Energy is conserved exactly, forever. The orbit never decays.
  • Absolute time. Every body shares the same clock. There is no frame-dependent tick rate, no time dilation between the three.

Each of these is not a minor correction. Each is a qualitative failure — a place where the Newtonian model doesn't approximate reality but describes a fundamentally different universe.

VI

Where the Loop Breaks

Section IV established the triangle: the state of body 1 depends on 2, which depends on 3, which depends on 1. That circular dependency is the fixed-point condition $x = f(x)$.

Newton's gravity doesn't respect this loop. It computes all forces simultaneously from a single global snapshot — an omniscient view that no body actually has. The information each body actually receives travels at $c$, arriving from where the other bodies were, not where they are.

$$\text{Newton: } \mathbf{r}_j(t) \quad \longrightarrow \quad \text{Reality: } \mathbf{r}_j\!\left(t - \frac{|\mathbf{r}_i - \mathbf{r}_j|}{c}\right)$$

For the figure-8 orbit this delay is negligible — roughly $10^{-8}$ seconds for solar-mass bodies at these separations. But the conceptual error is total. The coupling loop doesn't close instantaneously. The self-consistency condition $x = f(x)$ must account for the delay, and when it does, it forces the field to carry its own dynamics. The field becomes a participant, not a backdrop.

VII

What Self-Consistency Demands

The first-principles derivation reaches the rational field equation at Section VI:

$$N(p/q) = N_{\text{total}} \times g(p/q) \times w\!\bigl(p/q,\; K_0\, F[N]\bigr)$$

This equation refers to itself. The population at each frequency depends on the coupling, which depends on all other populations. Self-consistency — demanding the system's description of itself is consistent — resolves every Newtonian pathology:

  • Finite propagation. The fixed-point iteration converges at a finite rate. No signal outruns the iteration speed. At the continuum limit this becomes $c$.
  • Back-reaction. The coupling strength $K_0\, F[N]$ depends on $N$ itself. The field shapes the sources that shape the field. This mutual dependency is the content of $G_{\mu\nu} = 8\pi G\, T_{\mu\nu}$.
  • Regulated contact. Two oscillators at the same frequency lock — they don't diverge. The mediant $\frac{a+c}{b+d}$ interpolates; it never produces a pole. The $r \to 0$ singularity has no analogue in the discrete framework.
  • Radiation. The coupling loop has a finite relaxation time. Perturbations propagate as waves in the coherence field — gravitational waves, with the quadrupole structure forced by the rank-2 nature of $\gamma_{ij}$.
  • Relative time. Each oscillator's clock is its own phase $\theta_i(t)$. The coherence tensor $C_{ij} \to \gamma_{ij}$ defines local time. Different bodies in different coherence regions tick at different rates. This is gravitational time dilation.
VIII

The Three-Body Correction

Return to the simulation. The Chenciner–Montgomery figure-8 is a solution of Newton's equations. In full general relativity, the orbit doesn't close. The correction has three layers:

$$\Delta\phi = \frac{6\pi G M}{c^2 a(1-e^2)}$$

The first layer is perihelion precession. Each body's closest approach to the center of mass shifts by $\Delta\phi$ per orbit — the same formula that resolved Mercury's anomalous 43 arcseconds per century. For three equal masses on the figure-8, the precession accumulates symmetrically: all three orbits precess together, preserving the choreography but rotating it.

The second layer is gravitational radiation. Three bodies on the figure-8 are three accelerating masses with a time-varying quadrupole moment. They radiate energy. The orbit slowly inspirals. Newton predicts eternal stability; self-consistency predicts decay.

The third layer is the deepest. In Newton's formulation the three-body problem is chaotic — Poincaré's result — meaning initial conditions determine the outcome with unbounded sensitivity. But in the framework, the coupling loop has a finite relaxation time. Chaos persists, but the Lyapunov exponent is bounded by the propagation speed. Information about instability cannot spread faster than $c$. The chaos is real but causal.

This is the essential correction: the three-body problem in Newton's gravity is a system that refers to itself but has no mechanism to enforce consistency of that self-reference. The first-principles framework supplies exactly that mechanism — the self-consistency condition $x = f(x)$ realized as the field equations. The three-body problem is not solved by general relativity. It is regulated by it.
